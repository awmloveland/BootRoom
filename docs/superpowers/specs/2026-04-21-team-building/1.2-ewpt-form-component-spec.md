# 1.2 Drop ewptScore's Form Component — Spec
_Date: 2026-04-21_
_Umbrella: [../2026-04-21-team-building-review-design.md](../2026-04-21-team-building-review-design.md)_
_Status: Ready to implement_

## Problem

Form is double-counted in the team score. `wprScore` already weights form at 25% per-player, so `avgWpr` inside `ewptScore` already carries roughly 16% effective form content. The explicit `avgForm × 0.25` term in `ewptScore` stacks another 25% on top, giving form ~41% of the final team score — nearly double what the formula looks like on the surface.

This runs counter to the April 20 rebalance goal ("overall quality dominates") and introduces a secondary bug: guests and new players with `recentForm = ''` contribute 0 to `avgForm`, dragging the team score down just by being on the lineup.

## Goal

- Eliminate the double-counting — form should contribute to the team score only once, via each player's own WPR.
- Fix the guest/new-player drag as a side effect.
- Keep the relative weight between "overall quality" and "standouts" intact (65 → 10 becomes 90 → 10).

## Fix

In `ewptScore` (lib/utils.ts, around line 162):

1. **Remove** the local `avgForm` computation (around line 170).
2. **Remove** the `avgForm * 0.25` term from the final return expression (around line 189).
3. **Raise** the `avgWpr` weight from 0.65 to 0.90 (absorbing the freed 25%).
4. **Delete** the `playerFormScore` helper (lib/utils.ts:138–147) — it has no other callers.
5. **Update** the JSDoc comment block above `ewptScore` to reflect the new weighting.

Before / after of the final expression:

```ts
// Before
avgWpr * 0.65 + top2Avg * 0.10 + avgForm * 0.25 + gkModifier + varietyBonus + depthBonus

// After
avgWpr * 0.90 + top2Avg * 0.10 + gkModifier + varietyBonus + depthBonus
```

## Effective form weight — before and after

| Channel | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Form inside per-player WPR (25% of WPR × avgWpr weight) | 16% | 22% |
| Explicit team-form term | 25% | 0% |
| **Total effective form weight** | **41%** | **22%** |

## Illustrative example — form volatility

Hold avg WPR constant at 60 (top-2 average also 60) and vary average team form:

| Scenario | Before ewptScore | After ewptScore |
|---|---|---|
| Team with avg form 40 | 55 + mods | 60 + mods |
| Team with avg form 80 | 65 + mods | 60 + mods |
| Range of team-level form impact | **10 pts** | **0 pts** |

Form still affects team scores via each player's individual WPR — it's just no longer added a second time as a team-level term.

## Guest-drag regression check

Two 6-player teams with the same average WPR of 60 and top-2 average of 60. Team A is all rated with average form 50. Team B is five rated players + one guest at WPR 60 with empty form (same avg WPR, same top-2).

| | Team A (all rated) | Team B (1 guest) |
|---|---|---|
| avgWpr | 60 | 60 |
| avgForm | 50 | 41.7 |
| **Before ewptScore** (+ mods) | 39 + 6 + 12.5 = **57.5** | 39 + 6 + 10.4 = **55.4** |
| **After ewptScore** (+ mods) | 54 + 6 = **60.0** | 54 + 6 = **60.0** |

**Before:** the guest's empty form pulls the team down ~2.1 points despite matching on WPR. **After:** the teams score identically — no spurious drag.

In practice guests usually have different WPRs from rated players too, so their team may still legitimately score differently — that's not the drag bug, that's the model correctly reflecting the guest's strength.

## Test plan

**New tests in `lib/__tests__/utils.wpr.test.ts`:**

- Team of 6 rated players at avgWpr = 60 with variable avg team-form → `ewptScore` should be within ±1 pt across form levels 20 / 50 / 80 (team-level form should no longer amplify).
- Team of 5 rated + 1 guest at the same avg WPR as a team of 6 rated → `ewptScore` within ±0.5 pt. This is the guest-drag regression.
- Team of 5 rated + 1 new player at equivalent avg WPR → same-to-same regression.

**Updates to existing tests:**

- Any hardcoded expected value in `utils.wpr.test.ts` that was computed under the old formula. Walk each failing assertion, recompute using the new weights.

**Regression checks (should still pass unchanged):**

- `autoPick` property tests in `lib/__tests__/autoPick.test.ts` (they assert structural behaviour, not specific score values).

## Out of scope

- Changes to `wprScore`'s internal form component (the 25% weight inside per-player WPR is intentionally left alone — form still matters, just at the per-player level).
- Changes to the GK modifier, variety bonus, or depth modifier.
- Retroactive recalculation of stored `team_a_rating` / `team_b_rating` on past games.
- Any UI changes. The scale discontinuity between pre- and post-change ratings is accepted — raw scores aren't displayed numerically, only via `winCopy` pundit text.

## Observability after deployment

This is the biggest single behavioural change in the team-building review bundle. After merge, eyeball the first 3–5 real lineups post-deployment to confirm the split feels right. If team scores feel "too flat" (player differences compressed), the fallback dial is a partial redistribution — e.g., 85% avgWpr + 5% small team-form term — rather than the full 90%.
